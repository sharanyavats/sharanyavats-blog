Modeling the Future Challenge: Selected as Top 1% Project

December 15, 2025 | By Sharanya Vats

Achievement: My project "AI Data Centers' Carbon Emissions and Water Depletion in Metro Atlanta" was selected as one of three project highlights from 381 semifinalist teams in the Modeling the Future Challenge.

The Challenge

The Modeling the Future Challenge is a national actuarial science competition where high school students apply mathematical and statistical modeling to real-world problems.

This year, I chose to investigate an issue that connects my interests in technology and environmental science: the hidden water crisis caused by AI data centers.

The Problem

Everyone knows AI consumes massive amounts of electricity. But few discuss the water consumption.

Data centers require enormous amounts of water for cooling. In Metro Atlanta, where several major tech companies operate data centers, this creates sustainability challenges - especially during drought conditions.

My research question: How do we quantify the actuarial and environmental risks of AI data center water consumption under different climate scenarios?

My Approach

I combined several methodologies:

1. Data Collection: Gathered public utility data, climate projections, and data center energy usage patterns for the Atlanta region.

2. Actuarial Modeling: Applied risk assessment frameworks to quantify financial impacts under different scenarios.

3. Climate Analysis: Incorporated drought predictions and water supply constraints into the model.

4. Policy Implications: Analyzed how different regulatory approaches might affect outcomes.

Why It Was Highlighted

Out of 381 semifinalist teams, only three projects were selected as highlights during the program's national meeting. I believe mine stood out because:

• It addresses a timely, urgent problem that's underresearched
• It combines actuarial science with environmental analysis
• It has local relevance (Metro Atlanta) with global implications
• It demonstrates how mathematical modeling can inform policy decisions

What's Next

The next phase involves:

• Creating a detailed infographic (due February 2, 2026)
• Writing a comprehensive 25-page research report (due March 2, 2026)
• Presenting at regional finalist events (March 16-20, 2026)
• Competing for finalist status and scholarships

I'm working with a professional actuarial mentor who's helping me refine my methodology and strengthen my analysis.

The Bigger Picture

This project connects to my broader mission: using computational methods to understand and solve environmental challenges.

Whether it's AI's carbon footprint, water consumption, or future challenges in space sustainability, we need rigorous analytical frameworks to make informed decisions.

The Modeling the Future Challenge is helping me develop these skills while addressing a real problem that matters.
